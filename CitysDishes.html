★ Special to Tangier


Tagra

A traditional fish dish cooked in a clay pot with a rich tomato-based sauce and fragrant spices giving it a deep Mediterranean flavor.

Kaliente

A savory and soft chickpea flan, baked to perfection, commonly eaten as a warm, spiced snack in northern Morocco.

Bissara

A comforting and creamy fava bean or pea soup, typically enjoyed with a drizzle of olive oil, cumin, and fresh bread, during colder months.

Feqassa

A Moroccan take on the Spanish sandwich, often filled with tuna, eggs, olives, harissa, and fresh vegetables, making it a popular street food choice

Also in Tangier

Harrira

Harrira is a Moroccan soupe made with tomatoes, lentils, chickpeas, meat and spices, often served during Ramadan.

Boulfaf

A dish made of grilled lamb liver wrapped in thin strips of caul fat. Often enjoyed during Eid, it has a smoky, rich taste with a hint of cumin and salt.

Grilled Sardines

Grilled sardines marinated in chermoula, a Moroccan staple with bold flavors and a crispy char.

Lhem Bil Barqoq

A sweet-savory stew of tender beef or lamb with prunes, almonds, cinnamon, and saffron.
